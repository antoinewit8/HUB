## Design Context

### Users
Logistics and transport operations staff at Constant Bonivers Luxembourg SA and similar SMEs in the Greater Region (Luxembourg, Belgium, France). Users are dispatchers, fleet managers, and operations coordinators who work with the tool during active work shifts — checking fleet data, running route calculations, analyzing fuel costs, reviewing profitability reports. They are not developers. They use the tool on desktop, in an office or dispatch environment. Speed and clarity matter more than aesthetics. They don't have time to learn a complex UI.

### Brand Personality
Professional. Direct. No-nonsense. Think: a well-organized physical binder on a dispatcher's desk, not a Silicon Valley SaaS dashboard. Three words: **operational, reliable, dense**.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode. Used during working hours, in bright office environments.
- **Tone**: Utilitarian with precision — like industrial instrumentation or a well-designed logistics form. Not cold or clinical, but efficient.
- **References**: Dense data tables, clear status indicators, minimal chrome. Think GOV.UK design system, Bloomberg Terminal readability principles, classic transport management software.
- **Anti-references**: No purple gradients. No glassmorphism. No hero sections. No decorative illustrations. No rounded card grids with icons. Nothing that looks like a startup landing page.
- **Color**: Muted neutral base (slightly warm, not pure white). One strong functional accent (deep blue or slate) for interactive elements. Status colors (green/orange/red) used strictly for data meaning, never decoration.
- **Typography**: A condensed or semi-condensed sans-serif for data labels and table headers. A clean readable sans for body and UI text. NOT Inter. NOT Space Grotesk. Look toward typefaces used in print logistics, timetables, or industrial manuals — something with authority and density.

### Design Principles
1. **Data first** — every pixel serves the data. Decorative elements earn their place or get removed.
2. **Scannable at a glance** — operators need to spot anomalies (empty km ratio spikes, Friday alerts, overdue checks) in seconds, not minutes.
3. **No surprises** — UI behavior should be predictable. Confirmations before destructive actions. Clear status at all times.
4. **Dense but breathable** — pack information efficiently without creating visual noise. Use spacing to group, not to decorate.
5. **Color means something** — green/orange/red are reserved for status. Never use them as brand accents.
